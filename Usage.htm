    • Rules of Combat

    Othello or Reversi is played by two players, namely the Black and the White. They place double sided discs on a board, each disc has one of its side colored black and the other white. The player in turn must place one disc on the board, with his own color facing up. A disc can only be placed if there is an opponent's piece, or a row of opponent's pieces, that are surrounded by your discs. All of the opponent's discs between your discs are then flipped up side down to your color. One can capture discs in horizontal, vertical and diagonal lines even simultaneously. Thus it is possible to flip 18 discs in a single move, tough it is not certain that this is advantageous.

    The object of the game is to maximize one's final disccount. The player with the most discs is declared the winner. The two players move consecutively, if one has no legal moves than he must pass, otherwise moving is compulsory. The game ends when there are no legal options for either player, this may happen even if the board isn't filled.

    Unlike in chess, the right for the first move belongs to Black, however it is more advantageous to play White. Although the final disccount defines the winner, during the game this has no importance what so ever.

    User Interface

    Board Panel

    For your convenience, along the edges of the board you will find the name of the rows and columns, also all legal moves for the side in turn is highlighted.

    Info Panel

    Using a simple notation, the black player is noted with the plus sign while the white with the minus sign. For example, +d4 means a move for the black player on square d4; also, a game value of -34 means an expected outcome with a discdifference of 34 discs in the favor of the white player. An exclamation point means that a full depth search was performed and the game value vas precisely determined. The panel displays some data about the search and the game. These are the side in turn, the all moves played to this moment, the number of nodes visited during analysis, number of hits in the transposition table, the ratio between these two, number of null window research, search depth, and some other technical data. You can safely ignore this panel and still enjoy the game!

    Game Menu

    Game/New

    Starts a new game, if one is already started then a confirmation is required. You are given the choice between playing Black or White.

    Game/Abort

    During a game, you can use the command to abort the game.

    Game/Save Position

    Saves the current position, for later analysis.

    Game/Save Game

    Records on your hard disc, all moves made, in an easily readable ASCII format, for your bookkeeping purposes.

    Game/Load Position

    Loads a previously saved position, you are given the choice of playing Black or White.

    Game/Exit

    Instantaneously exits the program.

    Actions Menu

    (a few commands mainly for your enjoyment.)

    Actions/Hint

    Displays a hint (if available) about your best move, though the hint is resulted from a previous search it is quite improbable that you can win a game if all your moves are hinted in this way.

    Actions/Switch Sides

    If you feel that the situation is hopeless or lacks the challenge then you might try the game from the opponent's side. For example, you might switch sides in a lost endgame thus wining the match, however you will get no satisfaction from such victory.

    Actions/Undo

    If you feel that your last move wasn't what you wanted to be, then you can undo this move.

    Actions/Force Move

    Got tired by Shylock's lengthy thought process? Then you may force him to make his move, although a forced move is of a lesser quality.

    FAQ

    How to place discs?

    You are joking, right? Use the left click to place discs, all moves are highlighted.

    What about that strange board changes?

    Shylock doesn't cheat, double moves are allowed by the rules. When the opponent hasn't got any move options then he must pass, therefor Shylock can make multiple moves. You can track these by looking at the Info Panel's move list. Though the actual rules state that when one hasn't got any move options then (s)he must inform the other that (s)he passes, in order to reduce the communication overhead between the user, Shylock acts impolitely and simply informs you, with a flash of the application window and by an update on the Info Panel, that you had no options and he had made a double move. Depending on your machine and windows settings you may also hear short beep.

    How is the remaining time measured?

    Although the Info Panel updates are quite hectic, rest assured that the time is measured accurately, since the Info Panel only plays the role of an observer and not of a timekeeper.

    What does it mean "Timed out"?

    It means that Shylock had ran out of its time, thus following the spirit of time control you have won. I like challenges, and for this reason your time is not accounted for, so Shylock is in a more difficult situation since he has to play against an opponent who has unlimited time resources. Of course I am not being fair, for taking your chance of a clear victory by not allowing a game with equal terms. As soon as some of the other more urgent problems will be solved, an equal time match style will be implemented. Until then try to play with more search time like 15 or more minutes, there should be no problems this way.

    Other Othello programs

    There are many of them avaible on the net, some are strong some are not, some have nice graphics some dont (like Shylock). Perhaps the strongest Othello program out there is Logistello, who defeated the human world champion by a large margin. Programs like Hannibal, Zebra, Keyano, Kitty, TooThought!, Brutus (...) also have an amazing strength, some of these programs have versions avaible for download, most dont but you can play against them and strong humans too, on the GGS (Generic Game Server). It is a generally accepted fact that machines are better at Othello than humans players.
